# Trend Scan: 2026-02-11

## AI Agents & MCP
AI agents are shifting to autonomous 'digital coworkers' handling long-running tasks like full app builds (Anthropic 2026 report). Multi-agent systems focus on coordination for enterprise workflows (Google Cloud, Microsoft). MCP (Model Context Protocol, Anthropic Nov 2024) has exploded: multimodal (image/video/audio), async ops, chunked streams; adopted by Red Hat OpenShift, AWS Bedrock, Cursor IDE. Linux Foundation stewardship; predicted 2026 dominant standard replacing custom agents. A2A (Google) competes.

ArXiv: Moltbook (AI agent social platform Jan 2026); privacy management agents; self-evolving agents.

[Perplexity: https://news.microsoft.com/source/features/ai/whats-next-in-ai-7-trends-to-watch-in-2026/, Anthropic report; web: ruh.ai, machinelearningmastery.com, siliconangle.com]

## Video AI
Runway Gen-4.5 (Dec 2025) leads benchmarks (1247 Elo), with motion quality, native audio, multi-shot editing, character consistency. Gen-4 Turbo for fast shorts. $315M raise Feb 10 2026 at $5.3B val for world models (gaming/robotics). Outperforms Sora 2 Pro; no 2026 Sora/Kling updates.

## Competitors
**Opus Clip** dominates short-form: 20+ AI clips/video, Virality Score, auto B-roll/captions/reframing, YouTube-to-post. **Descript**: text-based editing, filler removal, Overdub, strong for podcasts but fewer clips (3-5). Opus for creators scaling social; Descript for precise long-form teams. Competitors: CapCut (effects), Captions.ai (premium), Riverside (recording).

## Creator Economy
**Diversified revenue & niche focus (2026):** Sponsored content 59% of earnings ($20.6B total social creator rev, +16%); platforms 24.4%, affiliates 8.2%. Shift to niche audiences over scale; long-term partnerships; content licensing as line item. Platforms: TikTok/YouTube/IG lead sponsorships, but podcasts (56% listeners prioritize), LinkedIn B2B, Amazon retail rising. AI reshapes discovery (chatbots/recs); production multiplier not replacer. Creator marketing budgets +171% YoY, core media allocation (71% orgs increasing).
